Frequently Asked Questions

How do I book a session?

You can book easily by filling out the contact form on this site, sending me an email, giving me a call, or even messaging via social media. I’ll get back to you quickly to find a time that fits your life.

What happens in the first session?

Our first meeting is just a gentle getting-to-know-you. There’s no pressure to share more than you want — it’s simply a chance to explore what brought you here and see if we feel like a good fit to work together.

How soon can I start?

Usually, I have availability within the week you reach out. I’m flexible with timings to help fit around your schedule, so we can get started as soon as you’re ready.

How long will counselling take?

There’s no one-size-fits-all answer. Some people find a few sessions helpful; others benefit from longer-term support. We’ll review things together regularly, and you’re free to pause or stop whenever you feel it’s right.

How much does it cost?

Sessions are £50 each, with a concessionary rate of £25 available for those who need it. Payment is usually made by bank transfer after each session.

What if I can’t afford therapy?

I understand therapy is an investment. If cost feels like a barrier, please talk to me. I offer a limited number of lower-cost spaces and can help guide you to other services if needed.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes. Everything you share stays between us, with a few important exceptions like safeguarding concerns or legal requirements — which I’ll explain clearly upfront so you always feel safe.

What kinds of issues do you work with?

I support people through many challenges, including anxiety, depression, grief, trauma, low self-esteem, relationship difficulties, identity questions, and men’s mental health. If you’re unsure, just ask — I’m happy to chat.

Do I need to prepare or bring anything to sessions?

No special preparation is needed. Just bring yourself and we’ll take things at a pace that feels comfortable for you.

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

Many people feel unsure at first. The best way is to try a session and see how it feels. There’s no pressure to continue if it doesn’t feel right — this is your journey.

What if I don’t feel comfortable?

Feeling safe and heard is key. If something doesn’t feel right, please let me know. Sometimes I might not be the best fit, and that’s okay — I’ll help you find someone who is.

Can sessions be held online or in person?

Yes, I offer both online and in-person sessions depending on your preference and availability. We can decide what works best for you.

What’s your cancellation policy?

I ask for at least 48 hours’ notice if you need to cancel or reschedule. If notice is shorter, there may be a charge unless there are exceptional circumstances.

Do you work with men, LGBTQ+ or neurodivergent clients?

Absolutely. My practice welcomes and supports people of all genders, identities, and backgrounds. I aim to create a safe, respectful space where everyone feels seen.
