You Do Not Need Permission

July 10, 2025

A heartfelt reflection on how grief and pain don’t require apologies — an invitation to just begin.

You sit down, shoulders hunched like guilt,

Lips are already forming the words:
“Sorry — I don’t talk about this much.”

And I nod, though inside I sigh.
Not at you.
At all the years that taught you
Your pain was something to preface.
To pad.
To apologise for.

You’re not a burden.
You’re a person.
With a past.
A knot of thoughts you’ve carried in silence,
afraid your story would take up too much space.

I’ve heard it a hundred ways:
“I’m not good at this...”
“Don’t want to waste your time...”
“Sorry, it’s probably stupid...”
And my heart gets heavier
Not from what you say
But from all you felt you couldn’t.

No one taught you
That grief has a right to speak
Even in your voice.

So here’s what I wish I could tattoo on the moment:
You do not need permission
To feel.
To speak.
To be heard.

No need to be polished.
No need to be strong.
No need to be sorry.

Just begin.

Ian Brown once said...

June 24, 2025

Reflecting on identity, growth, and holding onto pieces of who we are as we evolve.

Ian Brown once said, “It’s not where you’re from, it’s where you’re at.” I’d add: it’s also when you're at, and how you choose to live your life now.

I’m a bit of a fan of Ian Brown, The Stone Roses, and most things Madchester (for those that remember it), and although I can’t quite get into the flares anymore, I’ve still got a decent collection of bucket hats.

I used to think the story of who I am was mostly shaped by where I came from: the place, the time, the circumstances. But I now realise the real measure is in how I’m living now, the choices I make each day, the relationships I nurture, and the courage to keep growing, even when it’s uncomfortable.

I feel like I’m constantly being reminded that men often find it difficult to open up. To be fair, I see it in others, and I’ve felt it in myself. There’s this pressure to hold it all together, stay silent, and just power through. Sometimes, asking for help is seen as a weakness, but the truth is, there’s strength in honesty.

Being at this stage of life means realising that you don’t have to do it all alone. It’s about having the courage to ask for help when you need it, and showing up with intention, for your work, your family, and yourself.

It’s about letting go of perfection, letting in vulnerability, and finding purpose in the present, however messy or imperfect it may be.

And maybe, if we’re lucky, we reach a place where connection matters more than image. Where presence matters more than performance.

Still learning. Still growing. Still here — bucket hat and all.

After all, this is the one. And if The Stone Roses taught us anything, it’s that music, meaning, and second comings can echo for a lifetime.
